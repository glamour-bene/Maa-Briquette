Creating a Better Tomorrow

In Narok and Kenya at Large

George Mochu Karaya

Managing Director, Maa Briquettes

"After graduating from Kenyatta University, I found myself at a crossroads. Armed with a degree and a passion for environmental conservation, I sought a purposeful path. It was during this pivotal moment that I stumbled upon a YouTube video that would change my life forever. The video showcased donkeys laboring under heavy loads of firewood in a small town in Narok. Witnessing the relentless deforestation caused by traditional fuel sources, I felt compelled to take action. I envisioned a solution that would not only provide income but also protect the environment. And so, Maa Briquettes was born. In 2013, armed with determination and a laptop, I bootstrapped my venture. I sold the laptop to acquire a briquetting machine, kickstarting the production of eco-friendly charcoal briquettes. MAA Briquette became a beacon of sustainable energy, collecting agricultural waste and charcoal dust from the Narok environment. Our mission? To offer readily available and affordable fuel alternatives to households and hotels. My commitment didn’t stop there. I tirelessly worked to expand the company, exploring new feedstocks like macadamia nuts and bagasse. My vision extended beyond profit; it was about safeguarding the Maasai Mau Forest and promoting cleaner energy solutions. Today, as the managing director of Maa Briquettes, I continue to champion environmental stewardship while empowering my community at large."

Christine Resiato

Director, Maa Briquettes

So here’s to us —the flame keepers, the stewards of change. May our briquettes continue to light up the world, one glowing ember at a time!"
